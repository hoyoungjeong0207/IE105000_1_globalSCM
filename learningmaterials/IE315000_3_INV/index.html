IE315000 Supply Chain Management  |  Ch.11 Cycle Inventory · Ch.12 Safety Inventory · Ch.10 Bullwhip Effect

📋 Problem Overview

ChillMart Electronics is a consumer electronics retailer managing weekly replenishment for its best-selling 55″ smart TV. Demand is stochastic and your only levers are:
  • ROP (Reorder Point) — order when inventory position drops to or below this level.
  • Q (Order Quantity) — fixed lot size ordered each time.

Your score depends on both your own costs and how much variance you inject into the upstream supply chain (Bullwhip Effect). Large, infrequent orders reduce ordering cost but create demand spikes that force the distributor to hold expensive safety stock.

🏆 Scoring Formula

ComponentFormula
Holding Cost$2 × avg inventory × 52 wks
Ordering Cost$300 × number of orders
Backlog Penalty$15 × backlog units × weeks
Retailer CostSum of above three
Bullwhip Penaltymax(0, BR−1) × $5,000
Final ScoreRetailer Cost + Bullwhip Penalty

BR (Bullwhip Ratio) = Var(retailer orders) / Var(customer demand).
BR = 1 means no amplification (ideal). BR = 5 adds a $20,000 penalty.

💡 Hints

EOQ: Q* = √(2KD/h)
With K=$300, D=100/wk, h=$2:
Q* ≈ 173 units
Safety Stock (95% SL):
ss = z × σ × √L = 1.645 × 25 × √3
ss ≈ 71 units
Reorder Point:
ROP = μ×L + ss = 100×3 + 71
ROP ≈ 371 units

⚠️ These are textbook starting points. The bullwhip penalty may push optimal Q lower than EOQ. Use the Simulate tab to explore the trade-offs.
